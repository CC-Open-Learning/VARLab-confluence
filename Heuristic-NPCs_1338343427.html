VARLab : Heuristic NPCs

Created by Cam Turner, last modified on Oct 23, 2024

Start: Sept 9, 2024

PI: German & Cam

Team: Hayden & Alistair

Goal: For the purposes of enhanced story-telling, we want to explore autonomous, heuristic-based, NPCs

Story: “At what point do workers get in each others way and reduce throughput?”

Sprint 5-7:

  • Create 3 different NPCs that can all interact with each other:

    • Worker NPC: Retrieves an “order” walks around filling a pallet on a hand-truck and delivers it to the shipping door when complete.

    • Replenishment NPC: Uses a “forklift” to retrieve full pallets of products to refill empty bays on the ground floor. Blocks off traffic while in action and gets in the way.

    • Order/manager: Potentially NOT an NPC that is visible, but creates varied order lists comprised of various products that form complete pallets.

  • Warehouse:

    • Simulating a single work day at the warehouse.

    • Aisles are one-way in practice. Could have some of the workers ignore this and cause issues/chaos.

    • Trucks fill with 25 (or 50 if stacked two high) pallets. 13 on the right and 12 on the left.

    • Resources on a pallet are finite (16 items/pallet)

    • Warehouse can run out of a specific product, but 16 cartons per pallet, 2 pallets per level per bay and 4 levels per bay = 8 pallets of product per rack.

    • Max 144 products if one product per bay.

    • Shifts are 8 hours with two 15 minute breaks and a 30 minute lunch.

    • If we do different sized boxes, they would be multiples of size.

  • Worker NPC “Features”

    • Energy - Gets tired over time.

    • Initiative and Motivation - Some can be lazy, some eager. Varies across instances but generally constant per instantiation.

    • Speed - How fast can they walk? Should/could we have it decline with the “weight” of the pallet increasing?

    • Efficiency - Follows the list as printed, or optimizes it first? (as CE if most lists are already optimized)

    • “gives a shit” - How accurate they care to be. Do they care if they get the right boxes on the pallet or not.

  • Notes:

    • Boxes are called cartons in SCM
