VARLab : Velodyne Lidar Scanner

Created by Alistair Campbell, last modified on May 16, 2024

Findings

The Velodyne Ultra Puck we have is a laser imaging system. It’s pretty cool, but its art uses are largely limited. Velodyne tools are largely focused on autonomous vehicles and other AI based systems, and it would take a very large effort to convert this into data usable by a (non technical) person.

The setup process is somewhat cumbersome and requires a connection to a power source and an Ethernet cable (and a computer that can accept it).

The data generated is a point cloud in 3d space. While it can give a good impression of the physical space around the device it has many limitations in what it can “see.” Any obstruction will block its view of anything behind it. In our testing an entire wall was missing because its view was blocked by a monitor, so to generate a good pointcloud of a space, it would need to have a completely empty room where it could see all the walls.

The pointcloud data also only contains locations, and cannot be used for image data or anything of the sort. The best workflow would be to use it to get a scan of a space and have an artist recreate it by hand, which would be better served with some variety of measuring tape.
