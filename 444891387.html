Synopsis of User Feedback - [number] Responses Total

This is sample data, to give you an idea how to summarize UAT responses.

  • Most respondents (8/10) used a laptop/desktop computer. Few responses (2/10) used a Tablet/iPad. No responses used a mobile phone or other device.

  • Most respondents (9/10) identified themselves as being very experienced or experts at Bloom Restaurant.

  • ALL respondents indicated that they had no problem accessing or launching the DLS

  • Most respondents (9/10) indicated they had no problem navigating to the next module after completing a module. One user had problems when they maximized the display and did not know how to exit full screen mode.

  • All respondents rated the DTA as an effective and appropriate fit for this simulation. Minor complaints included that her movements were distracting, her lips did not match the words, and her facial expressions were too dramatic.

  • All respondents indicated that the right level of information was provided - not too much or too little. No suggestions were made about missing or unnecessary information.

  • Most respondents (8/10) said they did not encounter any issues that prevented them from moving forward or completing the DLS. One respondent accidentally exited the simulation and had to start from the beginning of the module (this is a known limitation of the VTS platform) and one respondent experienced a bug where the simulation stopped playing and they had to click a photo to make it start playing again. This bug has been repaired in the final release version (see https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/BK-215 for details about this fix).

  • All respondents indicated that the DLS is an accurate real-world depiction of the Bloom kitchen.

  • All respondents indicated that the quizzes were good and effective. Two suggestions for larger font size, although they did not seem to be specific to the quiz (but just font size in general, which is a known limitation of the VTS platform).

  • All respondents indicated that the DLS would effectively prepare students for in-person classes at Bloom.
