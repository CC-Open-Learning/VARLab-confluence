VARLab : Teleportation Stone Guide

Created by Brodin Collins-Robb, last modified on Feb 07, 2024

Welcome to VARniverse! This is a place where you can add a project of your own to a unique world. To do so, you will need to create and set up a teleportation stone, as this is how you can represent your work and mark your project into the main hub world!

NOTE:
Any project added to VARniverse MUST use the built-in render pipeline meaning projects using any other pipeline (URP, UDRP) will not work. Sometimes these projects can be converted to the built-in renderer but this is not always an easy task.

In addition, VARniverse is currently only setup to support 3D games and projects

Lastly, multiscene projects are supported by VARniverse as long as they follow the above criteria

 Instructions

We will begin the implementation of this stone from the TeleportationStone prefab…

  1. Find a spot in the VARniverse scene where you would like to place your teleportation stone.

  2. Navigate the projects tab to Assets->VARniverse Assets->TeleportationStone.

  1. From this folder, drag and drop the TeleportationStone prefab into the scene.

  1. Rename your TeleportationStone to the name of your project by right-clicking it from the hierarchy and selecting rename.

  2. In the hierarchy, drag and drop your TeleportationStone into the folder ‘MiniGame-Teleportation-Stones’.

  1. Navigate to the InfoCardImages folder in the project tab. (Assets->VARniverse Assets->TeleportationStone->InforCardImages)

  2. Import an image that represents your project by dragging and dropping your image into the folder you opened in step 7.

  3. Select the image you just dropped into this folder, and in the inspector window, change the texture type to 'Sprite (2D and UI)

  4. In the inspector window, again under the default section, change ‘Compression’ to ‘High Quality’.

  5. In the inspector window again, under the default section, check the ‘Use Chrunch Compression’

  6. In the inspector window again, under the default section, drag the ‘Compressor Quality’ to 100

  7. In the inspector window again, select ‘Apply’.

At this point your image should look something like the following…

  1. Expand the details of the TeleportationStone in the hierarchy by selecting the triangle on the left of the object's name. (Expand until you can see the Description, VAR, and IMG components)

  1. Select the IMG component from the hierarchy, and in the inspector tab in the ‘Sprite Renderer’ component, drag and drop the image you imported from steps 7-13 from the projects asset folder into the ‘Sprite’ area. (Your image will replace ‘noimage’)

Your image may be the correct scale but if not it will look something like the image above. If your image is the correct size skip step 15.

  1. In the inspector tab, while still selected on the IMG component of your image under the ‘Transform’ component and ‘Scale’, adjust the X and Y values until the image appears to be the correct size.

  1. From your TeleportationStones component in the hierarchy, find and select the ‘Description’ component.

  2. In the inspector window under ‘TextMeshPro - Text (UI)’ and ‘Text Input’ replace the ‘---’ with the name of your project, the ‘-' after ‘Author:’ with your name, and the ‘-’ after 'Semester:’ with the semester your project was from (ex. S23 where S = summer, F = fall, W = winter, followed by the year)

When adding images or descriptions for your stone you do not want to apply changes to the prefab if Unity presents this option as this will update the name and images for every teleportation stone in VARniverse!

  1. Save the project with ctrl+s or cmd+s
