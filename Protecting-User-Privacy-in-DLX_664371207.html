VARLab : Protecting User Privacy in DLX

Created by Stacey Dineen, last modified on Mar 29, 2023

To be fair, the ideal situation for VARLab is when our DLX do not know anything about our end users and where we let the LMS manage user access and authentication all by itself with no help from us! The vast majority of our DLX work this way, but there are a few isolated examples of learning experiences that do ask users to input information that could be construed as personally identifiable. At the time of writing this page, it is my understanding that all of our DLX, including those few outliers, are in compliance with the Protection of Privacy Policy at Conestoga College.

Copied from the Ontario Privacy Commission Fact Sheet “What is Personal Information?” dated October 2016:

Definition of “personal information” in the acts “personal information” means recorded information about an identifiable individual, including,

(a) information relating to the race, national or ethnic origin, colour, religion, age, sex, sexual orientation or marital or family status of the individual,

(b) information relating to the education or the medical, psychiatric, psychological, criminal or employment history of the individual or information relating to financial transactions in which the individual has been involved,

(c) any identifying number, symbol or other particular assigned to the individual,

(d) the address, telephone number, fingerprints or blood type of the individual,

(e) the personal opinions or views of the individual except where they relate to another individual,

(f) correspondence sent to an institution by the individual that is implicitly or explicitly of a private or confidential nature, and replies to that correspondence that would reveal the contents of the original correspondence,

(g) the views or opinions of another individual about the individual, and

(h) the individual’s name where it appears with other personal information relating to the individual or where the disclosure of the name would reveal other personal information about the individual

Examples of VARLab DLX that do not request or accept user input of personal information:

  • Bloom (all modules)

  • Motive Power 1.0 and 1.1

  • Trucking Pre-Trip Inspection

  • Heavy Equipment (all modules)

  • Precision Machining (all modules)

  • Firefighting: HazMat

Examples of VARLab DLX that do request and accept user input of personal information, in accordance with college privacy policy:

  • Firefighting: SAR 1.0 and 2.0 - The user is invited to enter their name and e-mail address, but this is not required to play the simulation. To bypass this screen, the user would need to enter at least 1 character in each field to proceed to the next screen. There is no statement or documentation available to the user to explain what the information is used for, but instructors explain to in-class users that the data is used to send DLX results (event log, screen shots, heat map, etc) to the email address entered. Instructors may enter their own email address, if they want to see the results instead of having them sent to the student.

  • CSI 1.0 and 2.0 - Same as the Search and Rescue sim.

  • Traffic Investigation 1.0 and 2.0 - Same as Search and Rescue sim.

These examples are not intended to provide a comprehensive or up to date list of all VARLab DLX. The references provided here are just examples, for illustrative purposes.
