Title: Theoretical basis of gamification: A systemic review and analysis of theory in research on gamification, serious games and game-based learning

Gamification- use of game elements in non-game contexts

It is an effective teaching strategy for enhancing learning.

Game elements- levels, points, badges, leader boards, avatars, quests, social graphs or certificates, feedback, progress

Gamification can

  • illustrate goals

  • nudge users in the guided paths

  • give users immediate feedback

  • reinforce good performance

  • simplify content to manageable tasks

  • promote motivational, behavior and learning outcomes

  • increase learner engagement, motivation and knowledge retention

  • promotes student’s creative problem-solving skills

Goals-

Focus on affect and behavopr:

Performance stats and tracking features of gamified systems may also support users in self-monitoring processes for self-defined goals, especially related to diet, excercise or medication

A learner might avoid playing if there is activity is associated with negative emotions and approaches to play the activity if it is associated with positive emotions.

Approach-avoidance Achievement Theory, Keller's motivational ARCS model, Self-determination theory
