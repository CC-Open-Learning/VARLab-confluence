Potential Solution

VR Onboarding

Content experts may face challenges with initial VR setup, leading to frustration

  • Create low-fidelity/whiteboxing assets in Blender for precision and speed before importing them into VR.

  • Set up an onboarding scene to get the CE familiarized with the software and reduce the steepness of the learning curve

Motion sickness and CE adoption to the technology

Time and effort in VR adoption -Lack of familiarity with VR tools reduces their ability to contribute effectively

Headset Tools

(Shapes XR) On-headset tools are slower to work with and less precise compared to the (Shapes XR) web tools

Whiteboxing in Blender is preferred over Shapes XR due to its greater precision and efficiency

  • ShapesXR can work for rough approximations, can be used by CEs or non-artists to convey ideas. Artists should take these ideas post-meeting and refine them into better looking and more accurate models

Headset Troubleshooting

Difficulty pointing to specific items on a collaboratorâ€™s screen during headset use creates inefficiencies.

  • Set up headset casting on a browser before handing over the headset to the CE, so we can see what they see

Wi-Fi Setup Problems with the headset

Persistent issues with Wi-Fi connectivity despite correct configuration.

  • IT/OPs / Team support

Controller incompatibility to batteries

Controllers are finicky with other batteries

  • Check that we have working fully charged batteries on the controllers before the session

Lack of audio stream through web app

For those participants joining through the web app, the lack of audio connectivity can hinder live collaboration.

  • Someone could share their screen on a call while they cast their headset to the web browser. The CE would be able to hear what other people are saying, but whoever is sharing the screen would act as middle person conveying the info the CE shares with the rest of the group.

Note-making

Making notes in the headset is a challenge

Using the web app with the lack of audio to make notes is an issue

  • Typing in VR is a general issue. There are bluetooth keyboards that can pair with headsets but people would have to walk around carrying them so still not great. Better to leave an asterisk or other type of sign and have someone take notes on a laptop regarding that particular marking, which can then be replaced via the web editor and turned into a proper comment element.

UI Prototyping challenges

Developing interactions iteratively requires time-intensive knowledge and prototyping, testing

with limited scenes on interactions
