VARLab : Student-Grade Hardware for Testing DLX (TURD)

Created by Stacey Dineen on Feb 04, 2025

Our development machines are generally much more powerful than the type of laptop or desktop hardware that most Conestoga students use. Our DLX need to be designed and tested using hardware that is equivalent to the type of machines that students in our target audience will be using, in accordance with the Program Device Requirements specifications.

At time of writing this page, CVRI does not have a wide range of test machines available, but we plan to grow this collection over time. These machines are called TURDs as a short form for True Usecase Rendering Devices (and mostly because it was just funny).

TURD1 - Dell Inspiron 13 3368 2- in -1 (circa 2016-ish)

OS

Windows 10 Home

Processor

Intel i7-6500U @ 2.5ghz 2 cores (released September 2015)
