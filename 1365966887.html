Guidelines for Observing a Usability Test: Dos and Don'ts

WHAT TO DO

WHAT NOT TO DO

 

 

Being friendly and smiling when talking to them

Interrupting them way too often while they are doing the task

OR chatting with them

Observe the reactions and make notes at what point do they feel - frustrated, happy or confused

Helping them click on what they are supposed to do

Asking them follow-up questions when you see a reaction or a struggle OR ask them if everything is what they were expecting

 

When they are stuck, ask them questions like:

  • Could you explain more on how you got here?

  • What do you think should be the next step?

When they are stuck,

  • oh very simple. here it is.

  • Do you think it is a good idea?

If they are still stuck and are not able to proceed, then

  • Do you see anything on the screen that would help with you what you are maybe looking for?

  • Do you think there was a better way to approach or an intuitive way to represent that?

  • I observed that you seemed to be looking for something on the screen, could you tell me more?

If they are still stuck and are not able to proceed, then

  • this is the button to click

  • is it a bad design to have that look?

  • is that confusing for you?

  • Did you notice that back button on the top left? was that intuitive enough?

When they provide feedback good or bad,

  • That is great feedback. that is very helpful

When they provide feedback good or bad,

  • Well,… we designed it that way because….

(justification for a design)

Having a poker face on any task they do

Expressions like being surprised or happy or congratulating on being able to successfully complete or saying

  • oh wow, nobody else got it but you

  • that is pretty obvious, you should have figured that out by now
