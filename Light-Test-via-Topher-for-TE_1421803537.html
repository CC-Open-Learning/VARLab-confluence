Overview and Purpose

For this test, we scanned one object, the light (whatever its name is and whoever its for) that Topher gave to us. The purpose of this test was to compare the time it took to produce the 3D model and the output result. The light was scanned and photographed with a black background in the lightbox set up. We also utilized the motorized turntable. Throughout the rest of this article we will present our findings.

Setup Review

This object was scanned and photographed during the Respiratory Therapy Test, so the setup review will be the same as what was presented in this article: https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/VAR/pages/1408892933/Respiratory+Therapy+Test?atl_f=content-tree

Hypothesis

We expected this object to give us some trouble due to the shininess of the metal; we did however, expect it to work fairly well otherwise. We thought that the black background would contrast well with the otherwise very light colours in the light and that the opaque diffuser part of the light would provide something solid for the software to grasp.

Conclusions

The black background did not assist the software in differentiating between the object and the background, and the reflection on the metal provided a darker texture to the base of the lap than what it looked like. It was surprising to see that the symmetrical nature of the light made it extremely difficult to capture, both in photogrammetry and 3D scanning. The top part of the light is an opaque white, which should have made it easy to scan, but it did not, and almost none of the white diffuser parts of the light were captured in photogrammetry. In 3D scanning, the software could pick up the white part, but due to the lack of object geometry complexity, it lost its tracking immediately and started spinning rapidly. This test went surprisingly poorly. It might help to add markers (potentially the shapes) to the object next time to help the tracing software pick up on the object's orientation better.

In conclusion, neither of the scans would be usable in any 3D environment and could not be used as a reference for asset developers to model. The photos might provide a good reference point to start from, but at that point, the lightbox setup might not be necessary, and we would only need to capture 4-15 shots to provide an accurate visualization of the object. We will talk to Topher for further opinions and advice, which will be provided below when acquired.
