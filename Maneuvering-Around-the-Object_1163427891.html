VARLab : Maneuvering Around the Object

Created by User 96d6e, last modified on Jul 04, 2024

What to Remember…

It is usually (almost always) a good idea to plan and coordinate the capturing session beforehand.

As always the more photos the better.

  • More detail is always better + sometimes photos don’t come out perfectly (blur is a common problem).

What to do….

The following images help to visualize how one should go about capturing in multiple different scenarios.

**Screenshots are from the Agisoft Metashape PDF (linked below)

When Capturing an Isolated Object

Ex: a crate, a fire extinguisher, etc. (this is our most likely use case scenario)

  • capture the object from multiple angles

  • typically the more object overlap in the images the better

    • don't go too crazy though

When Capturing a Long Horizontal Object

Ex: a wall or a row of lockers

  • Move along the object to capture the entirety of it, rather than pivoting from one point

    • this helps lessen the distortion created from perspective

When Capturing a Large Area

Ex: a room

  • move around the area in a circle or oval like motion with the camera pointing into the center of the room, rather than going to multiple points and capturing that area by pivoting around
