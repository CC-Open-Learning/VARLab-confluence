VARLab : Using a Turntable

Created by estronge, last modified by User 96d6e on Jul 04, 2024

This page exists to give additional tips and guidelines to follow when scanning using a turntable.

Avoid rotating the turntable at the same time that you are taking a photo. It is beneficial to find a clock-like rhythm of:

  • Take picture

  • Rotate turntable slightly

  • Stop, move hand

  • Repeat

Other things to note:

  • Ensure that your subject is as close to perfectly centered as possible to avoid stretching

  • Make sure turntable + background have high contrast with the subject

  • Markers on the turntable cover are present to give Agisoft more transformation data when creating the model. This helps in cases where the subject looks visually similar from many different angles, markers reduce blurring/distortion of the model and texture

(Note: before this page is complete, take images during our scan process and insert them in here)

We have a pretty standardized method to capture as much detail in a subject as possible. It is outlined as follows:

  • Align camera with a chosen marker, note where you are starting

  • Start from top down perspective, do not spin turntable, take images while moving camera down and away from subject until the entire turntable is in the frame

  • 1 full rotation back to starting marker

  • Lower camera slightly

  • 1 full rotation back to starting marker

  • Lower camera to front view of subject

  • 1 full rotation back to starting marker

  • Take images while slowly move to a more close-up view of the subject (not too close or images wonâ€™t be able to align correctly in Agisoft)

  • 1 full rotation back to starting marker

And that is it! This approach has given us consistent results aside from outliers that lose quality due to other reasons

Note that this process is primarily suited for use with the medium sized white box
