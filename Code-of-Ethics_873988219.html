VARLab : Code of Ethics

Created by Sana Javeed, last modified on Dec 05, 2023

This code of Ethical principles will guide the professionals involved in the User research to ensure that their work meets the highest ethical standards.

On this page:

  • Respect and Positive affirmations
  • Do No Harm
  • Right to be Informed
  • Right to Withdraw
  • Valid and Reliable data
  • Respect Privacy, Confidentiality, and Anonymity
  • References

Respect and Positive affirmations

  • Participants shall be treated with respect

  • Ensure the participants are aware that the product is being tested and not them. Affirm that before and during the study.

Do No Harm

  • Do not expose participants to any physical, emotional or mental stress.

  • Participants have the right to take a break at any time

  • Take reasonable steps to avoid any harm to the participants

  • Review for any needs when working with the elderly, people with disabilities and children. Precautions taken to avoid risks associated with such groups shall be clearly identified and reviewed.

Right to be Informed

  • Participants have the right to know about the goals and the detailed procedure of the study.

  • Ensure participants provide informed consent for any data collected during the study.

Right to Withdraw

  • Participants have the right to withdraw at any time of the study without penalty

Unless they have a signed contract with VARLabs that excludes them from withdrawing from the study

Valid and Reliable data

  • Ensure the data you collect is free from bias, accurate and valid

Respect Privacy, Confidentiality, and Anonymity

  • Do not reveal information that identifies participants without their permission and shall take reasonable precautions to avoid such information from being disclosed unintentionally.

  • Ensure the participants are given pseudonyms in the study.

  • Never disclose notes or reports in any public forum about participants unless disclosure is both legal or they have the expressed the permission to disclose.

References

Source : Baxter, K., Courage, C., & Caine, K. (2015). Understanding Your Users A Practical Guide to User Research Methods, Tools, and Techniques. San Francisco: Elsevier Science & Technology.
