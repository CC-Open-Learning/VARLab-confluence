VARLab : General Release DEV Checklist

Created by Cam Turner, last modified on Apr 10, 2025

This is intended to capture the current “state of the art” for the requirements for a General Release DLX. As software is completed teams/PIs can copy this list to their respective project’s Confluence section and ensure completion. This is NOT meant to describe all of the testing, validation, or other documentation that goes into the development. This is just the non-code TODOs that come after we have a final build but before we close out the project.

NOTE: Writing it here does not guarantee that all prior DLX that have GR builds have the entirety of these resources. This is a forward-looking checklist.

NOV 18, 2024 - THIS IS A DRAFT/BRAINSTORM. This is not yet authoritative as REQUIRED.

Every DLX release should include the following:

  1. A final SCORM build of the DLX

    • Stored in Sharepoint

    • Loaded into a Model Course Shell

    • VR Build for the VARDeck “VIP” events?

    • Other places?

  2. Instructor Materials

    • Orientation Video with voice over, talking about navigation, intended use and how to support the learners. [Used car sales voice] “Congratulations on your newest purchase! You might be wondering what features are present in your new DLX and why you’d want to use it in your class…”

    • Model course shell

    • Release Notes and Information

    • Power Point for instructor to use to introduce DLX to students

    • Navigation quick reference guide?

    • Debrief Questions?

  3. Student Materials

    • Orientation Video, with voice over, talking about supported hardware and various tips and tricks (same as Instructors is fine). This might be replaced with an IN-DLX tutorial instead. (Example: AMB Milstone here)

    • Student Reference Guide (in the form of a FAQ?)

  4. Promotional Materials

    • Raw gameplay video footage for future videos

      • Approx 60-90 seconds of engaging gameplay per clip - ok to record multiple clips 

      • Focus on showing off the artwork and the workflow from a student perspective

      • No vfx, no titles or informational panels, no music, prefer no in-game sfx or voiceover.  

      • These video clips are primarily used in various presentations, often compiled together with clips from other DLX.  For this reason, including sound, text, etc. can be more of a problem than a help, because they need to be stripped out for compilations.

    • “Sizzle” Reel

      • 90-120 second video that includes CVRI title and text “captioning” that could be played on a loop (or in a playlist on a loop) at a “tradeshow booth” style event. Must be usable without audio.

    • Other stuff?

  5. Production Analytics

    • Confirm the dashboard is updating with production analytics

    • Ensure reporting is all wired up properly (what does this even mean?)

  6. Documented release certification information

    • Confirmation that comprehensive testing (GRID) has been completed

    • Trackable, verifiable test report (maybe with links) against the final release build from the Dev team

  7. CE/Department Sign offs

    • [Guess] Email or other written acceptance of the GR build for classroom use… from who?

    • [Guess] Proper video recording of the GR acceptance during the last CE meeting?

    • Presentation at the next scheduled Program Advisory Committee for the program?

    • What really needs to happen to “accept” this from the client point of view?

  8. Internal Project Parking

    • Backlog is as accurate as we can make it. Be kind to whoever eventually comes back to do a v2

    • All Sprints are closed off properly.

    • All known issues are reported in Jira, even if there is no plan to resolve them.

    • Team has “brain dumped” in all the appropriate ways before being re-assigned to other projects.

    • Relevant issues are reported in the Release Notes for future quick reference.
