VARLab : DSLR & Phone Camera Capturing Tips/Techniques

Created by User 96d6e, last modified on Jul 03, 2024

Agisoft Metashape is typically used with landscape photos, however, portrait orientation might be best for some objects.

DSLR/Mirrorless Camera

  • Capture Format: RAW converted to TIFF (avoid JPG)

    • JPG has less information (lower quality) and the compression may create more noise

  • ISO: On the lowest value possible (not at the expense of capturing the object)

    • a high ISO results in more noise/grain in the image

  • Aperture Value (F value): :aperture: should be high, or at least high enough for sufficient focal depth.

    • A lower value results in more blur to different depths.

      • Eg: Only a portion of the object will be clearly in focus, and varying levels of blur will be across the rest of the object.

  • Shutter Speed: should be high/fast to avoid blur which can occur from slight movements. Value will most likely be 100 minimum, especially in the lightbox.

  • Focus: :focus: Most of the time auto-focus will suffice, manual focus might be necessary depending on the object and scenario.

  • Aim for the highest possible resolution

Phone Camera

There is usually minimal control over a phone’s capture settings, and it is typically less efficient on phone than on camera.

Therefore the basics would be:

  • Tap on the phone’s screen to focus on the specific object

  • Try to hold the phone still while capturing

  • Avoid taking photos while repositioning
