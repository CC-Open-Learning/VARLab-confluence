VARLab : NPC Characteristics

Created by Alistair Campbell on Sept 18, 2024

Some possible variables that could influence NPC behavior. NPC’s could have this value set which would determine how they behave in the world.

  • Motivation

    • Could also be described as professionalism or maturity

    • Would impact how diligently they carry out their tasks, likelihood of getting distracted or distracting others, or the time it takes to transition from one task to another

    • Motivation could be influenced by world factors. Being watched closely by a manager/overseer type of entity could increase motivation temporarily

  • Skill

    • Impacts how effective the NPC is at carrying out their tasks.

      • Higher skill NPCs may be able to complete an action faster

      • Low skill NPCs could make mistakes requiring tasks to be redone

      • Low skill NPCs could be more likely to become frustrated from a task, especially if making mistakes

    • Talking implementation for a minute, skill could be represented on a task as a “skill required” value. An NPC with less than the required skill would have a chance to fail the task, increasing frustration (explained in temperature point). NPC teams would benefit from a combined skill level, allowing them to overcome difficult tasks.

      • If an NPC encounters a task with too high of a skill requirement, it could be given a decision to get help vs attempt it themselves.

    • Skill could be influenced by working as a team

      • This opens the door to multi-person tasks for NPC’s. “Lift that heavy box” could be a 2 person task, and it would open up the door for interesting interactions/cases like an NPC going to get help with their task from another NPC.

  • Temperature/mood/frustration

    • Value that could drive how they interact with others and the world

    • value could be influenced by events in the world, as well as having a baseline set

      • Things like having their current task interrupted could raise frustration values

      • If someone repeatedly bumps into them/gets too close while they are trying to work, they may get more frustrated over time as the interruptions persist

    • frustrated NPC’s could be prone to making more mistakes due to clouded judgement or rushing

    • Extremely frustrated NPCs may abandon their tasks in order to cool down

    • Dialog could be influenced by NPC frustration. Frustrated NPC’s may have more aggressive dialog options when interacting with players/other NPCs

    • Overcoming a challenging (high skill requirement, see skill section) could provide a temporary mood boost. This could create opportunities for cases like a well managed warehouse resulting in high morale due to successful execution of difficult tasks.
