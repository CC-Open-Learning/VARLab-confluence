Triplanar shaders are for objects that do not rely on having UV coordinates unwrapped and assigned. The most obvious and most ubiquitous implementation will be walls, floors, and ceilings, though these shaders can certainly be used in other places.

The Triplanar shaders we have rght now come in two flavours: The Triplanar Triple Mask shader and the Triplanar XYZ Shader.

The triple mask shader has identical inputs and settings as the MRO Universal Triple Mask shader. If you’re looking on information for the Triple Mask version of the triplanar shader then for now take a look at this page:

General MRO Shaders

If instead you’re looking for information on the Triplanar XYZ Shader, then continue reading!

The Triplanar XYZ Shader

There are three large differences between the the Triplanar Triple Mask and the Triplanar XYZ shaders:

  1. The XYZ Shader does not have a masking feature, meaning that it cannot be used to quickly create a colour variation of the material.

  2. The XYZ Shader can have three separate materials being projected with it - one for front-back, one for left-right, and one for top-bottom. Additionally, each of these materials can be rotates independant of the other two.

  3. The XYZ shader is able to access the object’s local space, meaning that the texture will stay in line with the object when being rotated or moved without causing weird distortions.
