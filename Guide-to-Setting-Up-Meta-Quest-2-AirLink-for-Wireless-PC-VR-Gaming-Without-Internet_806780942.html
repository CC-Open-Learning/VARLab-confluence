VARLab : Guide to Setting Up Meta Quest 2 AirLink for Wireless PC VR Gaming Without Internet

Created by User 2f6b4, last modified on Sept 20, 2023

Prerequisites:

  • Oculus Quest: Ensure that your Oculus Quest is updated to the latest firmware.

  • PC: Confirm that your PC meets the Oculus AirLink requirements.

  • Router: You'll need a Wi-Fi router that supports both 5GHz and 2.4GHz bands. For a more stable connection, connect your PC to this router using an Ethernet cable. In this case, we are using a TP-Link Router.

Steps:

1. Download and Install the Oculus App:

  • On your PC, open a web browser and go to the Oculus website (https://www.oculus.com/download_app/?id=1582076955407037). You will need internet for this step.

  • Download the Oculus app for your PC.

  • Follow the on-screen instructions to install the Oculus app.

2. Log In to the Oculus App:

  • Launch the Oculus app on your PC.

  • Click "Log In."

  • Enter your Oculus account credentials from Bitwarden.

  • Follow the on-screen prompts to complete the login process.

3. Connect Quest to Wi-Fi:

  • Put on your Quest headset.

  • From the home menu, navigate to 'Settings' > 'Wi-Fi.'

  • Connect your Quest to the same Wi-Fi network as your PC. (In this case, we are using the TP-Link Router with the Wi-Fi password "metaquest2"). No internet connection is required.

4. Connect PC to Router:

  • Ensure your PC is connected to the same Wi-Fi router using Ethernet.

5. Enable AirLink on Quest:

  • Put on your Quest headset.

  • From the home menu, navigate to 'Quick Settings' by pressing the 'Oculus' button on your controller.

  • Select 'Quest Link.'

  • Toggle on 'AirLink.'

6. Connect to the Alienware Laptop:

  • Once AirLink is enabled, you should be able to connect to your Alienware laptop.

7. Start Using AirLink:

  • Once connected, your PC VR library will be accessible from the Quest's main menu.

  • Launch any PC VR game or app, and it will stream to your Quest wirelessly.
