VARLab : Blender VR Scene Inspection Addon Guide

Created by Ian Kennaley, last modified on Dec 19, 2023

THIS ARTICLE IS IN DRAFT


The purpose of this document is to provide a clear and easy to follow guide for how to use Blender’s VR Scene Inspection addon with VARLabs Quest 3 VR Headsets.

Screen Casting Note: If there is a need to use Screen Casting from the Headset so that onlookers can view what is being seen by the Headset wearer, please view the following guide for quick setup: Cast to a screen with Meta Quest | Meta Store (it is recommended to use a Link Cable)

Step 1: Ensure that your Quest 3 Headset is set as the Active OpenXR Runtime headset by Opening the Oculus App, Navigating to the Settings Tab and then under the General Tab click on “Set Oculus as active”

Step 2: Open the Blender production file with asset(s) that you wish to inspect with a VR headset on

Note: Be sure to confirm with the asset developer(s) who made the scene to ensure that all model and material elements have been correctly set up for rendering

Step 3: Enable the Blender VR Scene Inspection Addon by going through the following:

i: Edit → Preferences

ii: In the Preferences Window, go to the Add-ons Tab and ensure that the addon “3D View: VR Scene Inspection” is enabled and then close the Preferences window

iii: Press N on your keyboard to open the Properties panel, and then at the bottom of the tabs click on the VR tab and verify that the Addon enabled successfully (dont do anything yet - a bit more setup to do)

Note: Ensure that the settings of the tab are set the same as pictured above - with the exception of the Camera Clipping Settings, those are likely to require some tweaking, especially when viewing small objects (such as a pencil)

Step 4: Hook up your Quest3 VR Headset to your computer and ensure you are using a Link Cable for best performance

Step 5: Once the Link between your Computer and Headset is active you should then bring the virtual Monitor up that corresponds to the Computer Monitor you have Blender open on

Step 6: Once the Blender Monitor is open, click on the “ > Start VR Session” button and this should start the Addon

Step 7: You should now be presented with a view of the 3D Space and you should be able to use your controllers to move around the space. The controls are as follows:

- Left Hand Controller Stick: Forwards, Backwards, Strafe Left, Strafe Right
- Right Hand Controller Stick: Fly Up (Forward), Fly Down (Backwards), Rotate Right, Rotate Left
- Right and Left Triggers: Press and Hold to activate a Teleport pointer (Teleport to the nearest object the Pointer points at). Release trigger to teleport
- X/A Button: Resets camera to the 0 position of the Z axis (not world center, just wherever the camera is currently positioned)

Step 8: Once you are finished viewing the scene, push your headset up and from within the Blender application window on your Computer Monitor (not in headset) to ensure the Addon stops running → In testing, the App is memory heavy and introduces some heavy screen chopping when trying to stop the Addon from within the Headset view.

Once the addon has stopped running, dump your PC’s RAM memory as the addon uses a large chunk of memory and this will result in overall poor performance of your computer without doing so (a computer restart of course also corrects this so no longterm concerns).
