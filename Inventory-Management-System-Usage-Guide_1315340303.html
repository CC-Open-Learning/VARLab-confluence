VARLab : Inventory Management System Usage Guide

Created by User a3ba7 on Sept 04, 2024

Overview

The Inventory Management System streamlines the tracking and retrieval of items such as cables, connectors, and hubs, both in-stock and checked-out. Users can remotely search for specific items and verify their availability before visiting the IT room at 2C12. This improves asset visibility, reduces unnecessary trips, and significantly enhances efficiency in resource management and allocation.

Prerequisites

You can access the website via this link and begin using it immediately. However, some features such as submitting order requests for out-of-stock items require an account.

During the onboarding process, new team members will receive an automated message in MS Teams with instructions for setting up their account. If you do not receive this message, please contact IT for assistance.

Usage Guide

To search for a connector, for example USB-C to DisplayPort (DP). You can select USB-C as input and DisplayPort (DP) as the output as shown below:

Due to the difficulty in identifying the input and output parts of a connector, you can easily swap the inputs and outputs using the button to ensure that the connector you are looking for available.

By selecting at least one input and one output, the system will automatically search the inventory and provide results based on the following:

  1. Direct Connector Found: The system locates a connector that matches your search criteria, as shown below:

  1. Indirect Connector Found: If a direct connector is not available, the system will suggest a similar connector composed of 2-3 parts, as shown below:

  1. No Connector Found: If neither a direct nor indirect connector is available, the system will display a message and offer the option to submit an order request by clicking the button:

If the system successfully finds a direct or indirect connector that you are satisfied with, please visit the IT room at 2C12. An IT team member will assist you with checking out the connector.
