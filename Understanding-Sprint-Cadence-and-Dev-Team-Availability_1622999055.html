VARLab : Understanding Sprint Cadence and Dev Team Availability

Created by Stacey Dineen on Feb 12, 2025

For team members that are outside of the software development process, it can be difficult to identify the best time to plan an event or big meeting. If a meeting or event is accidentally planned at a time when development teams are unavailable, participation will be lower than expected and engagement opportunities will be missed.

Here is a general guide to typical sprint cadence for Production Project Teams (other teams may have different objectives or routines, so please take this with a grain of salt).

Typical development sprints are 10 days long, so I have used those labels for identifying parts of the sprint. You’ll need to adjust accordingly for situations where statutory holidays or project parameters require a sprint that is more or less than 10 days long.

Day 1 is always the first day of the sprint, and days 8,9, and 10 are always the last 3 days of the sprint. If you need to adjust sprint length, the expansion/contraction point will be in the Days 2-7 part of the sprint.

DAY 1

Sprint Planning Dev team members including DNA are generally unavailable on this day of each sprint, please avoid scheduling any meetings or events unless it is an emergency.

DAYS 2 - 7

Dev Days These are regular work days and a meeting or event of up to 3h should be able to be accommodated without having too much impact on sprint goals. Note that any meeting longer than 1 hour MUST be booked BEFORE Sprint Planning day, so that the subtraction can be included in the sprint plan.

DAY 8

Last Dev Day This is similar to days 2 - 7, but with the crucial difference that it is the final dev day before code freeze. Dev team members may be available to participate in meetings or events on this day, depending on how well they have progressed on their sprint goals. If they are racing to achieve sprint goals before code freeze, they will not be available on this day.

DAYS 9 and 10

Code Freeze and Demo Dev team members including DNA are generally unavailable on these final 2 days of each sprint, please avoid scheduling any meetings or events unless it is an emergency.
