VARLab : Creating Promotional Videos for DLX

Created by Balazs Karner on Apr 18, 2024

Getting Started

In an effort to re-use as much content, and work as possible for future projects, all videos should be created in the same software. ShotCut is a free, open-source, cross-platform video editor that allows professional video creation with an easier learning curve due to it being more lightweight. If you are familiar with basic video editing, but not with ShotCut, check out this YouTube playlist for tips, transitions, and filters. All recordings and project files should be stored in the VARLab Promotional Materials SharePoint folder for use later.

Before beginning to record footage, create a storyboard to plan your video scene by scene. PowerPoint and Figma are both good tools to storyboard your promotional video. A template for storyboarding can be found here. The goal with a storyboard is to pre-plan your video with images or illustrations in sequence before diving in to record every moment of every DLX. An example below shows how the 2024 E3 trailer was storyboarded.

Free to use music can be found at various websites, however, Pixabay has a good selection of positive, upbeat, promotional product music. Check the recorded footage from previous trailers on SharePoint to see if it is usable before jumping in to re-record all your footage. A quick reminder that if you are making a video for a VARLab-developed DLX, you can clone the repo and alter the project to get the cinematic shots you want with Cinemachine.

Next Steps

Acquiring Footage

Third-party vendors(Calian/XpertVR) should provide trailers for their release candidates. We cannot add camera pans and dollys via Unity because we do not have their source code. Additionally, VARLab DLX that reaches a Milestone or GR target should be released with a brief(~1 minute) demo reel of everything the devs want to show off, such as complex systems, features, high-quality textures, etc, as well as a playthrough of the sim from start to finish. Devs just finishing the project are much more familiar with the exciting parts of the DLX and how to show it off than a team creating a promo video for VARLab X months in the future

Lessons Learned

Recording Footage

VARLab developed DLX

  • To get smooth camera movements in your recordings, download the project files and set up a camera dolly using Cinemachine.

Externally developed DLX

  • Set up a guide rail for your mouse with the straight edges of a cardboard box, piece of wood, etc. Some externally developed DLX have very fast character movement and/or high default camera sensitivity with no way to adjust it in the sim, making smooth pan shots difficult.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q1fkx94vHtg

You can see a comparison of when a guide rail wasn’t used for HazMat’s original recording in this clip and when it was in this clip.

Project Structure

Keep your folder structure the same when collaborating with editors. Otherwise, when opening the project, ShotCut cannot locate the files you are using. Consider creating a directory in your root drive that can be shared across machines, such as C:\Promotional Video\.

Collaborating

When collaborating on a video, it might be easier to split it into different sections and have each member create a rough version before merging it into a master project file. ShotCut allows you to copy the timeline from one project directly into the timeline of another project, including transitions and filters.
