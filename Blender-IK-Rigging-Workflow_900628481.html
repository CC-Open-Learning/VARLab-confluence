Step 1: Ensure your FK rig is properly set up

Ensure that once you have completed an FK Rig that you have done the following:

  • Created a Root Bone

  • Set All Parenting Relationships

  • Connected Bones in Chains as needed

  • Set all Bone Names

  • Ensured all Weights are Painted Correctly

Step 2: Create Target Bone for the bone_Flap_Bottom_Left Chain

For the purpose of giving the IK chain a Target to point at you should add a new Unconnected bone at the position of the Head Orb of the last Bone in a Bone chain. In Edit Mode on the Rig Create the unconnected Target Bone for the bone_Flap_Bottom_Left chain by placing the 3D cursor at the Head Orb of bone_Flap_Bottom_Left.003 and then add a new Bone to the Rig at the position of the 3D cursor with Shift + A and then bring the Head Orb down so that the bone is much less large, then rename the Bone as bone_Flap_Bottom_Left_IKTarget

Step 3: Add the Inverse Kinematic Bone Constraint to bone_Flap_Bottom_Left.003 in Pose Mode

Now that the IKTarget bone has been created its time to add the Inverse Kinematic Bone Constraint to the last Bone in the chain. To do this - Select bone_Flap_Bottom_Left.003 and then switch to Pose Mode and in the Bone Constraints tab add the Inverse Kinematics constraint, set Target to your Rig and then set Bone to the IKTarget Bone â†’ bone_Flap_Bottom_Left_IKTarget

Alternate Bone Constraint Creation Workflow

An alternate way to create the Bone Constraint Setup is to select your IKTarget and Last Bone in Chain together in Pose Mode and with the Last Chain Bone as the active Selection you can add the Inverse Kinematics Bone Constraint and it will automatically target the Rig and IKTarget bone.

Workflow: Left Click IKTarget Bone â†’ Shift + Left Click Last Bone in Chain â†’ Press Shit + i â†’ Add IK â†’ To Active Bone â†’ Left Click Bone Constraint Tab â†’ Confirm IK Bone Constraint creation with Target and Bone correctly set

Step 4: Setting Bone Properties for Chain Bones affected by Inverse Kinematic Setup - Lock Full Rig in Place and then Select the First Bone in your Chain

We can configure the movement constraints of the Chain of Bones being affected by this Inverse Kinematic setup so that they move correctly fairly easily. First we need to ensure the Entire Rig is Locked in Place for the IK so that we can better Isolate the the first Chain for testing: Select the first Bone in the Chain and then click on the Bone Properties tab in the Properties Panel and then expand the Inverse Kinematics Nested Options and then enable the display of Axes in the Viewport Display Nested Options of the Object Data Properties Tab so that you can Visually see the Local XYZ Pole Vectors, and then Select all Bones with A and then Lock the X/Y/Z Movement of all the bones in the Bone Properties tab Inverse Kinematics Nested Options Lock IK Fields and then Right Click each Lock Icon and Left Click on Copy to Selected to copy that setting to the rest of the Bones in the Rig â†’ Be sure to Check that the Settings were successfully Copied by clicking on a random Bone on the Rig and verify that the X/Y/Z Locks are enabled

  • Important Note: It is best to only Lock the entire Rig in place when you are about to configure the Constraints for the first time - as you configure the Movement constraints on the remainder of the Rig you will need to adjust the Locks according to each Bones individual Local Pole Vectors

  • Note: For the Green KED Splint subject of this guide, we want the bones to only rotate on the world Y axis (in relation to the Orientation of the overall 3D scene, not the local Pole vectors) and thus must manually set these constraints according to their Local Pole Vectors in the Lock IK X/Y/Z Fields of the Bone Properties Tab Lock Icons that Correspond to the Local Pole Vectors that need to be Locked for each Bone in the Chain

Step 5: Setting Bone Properties for Chain Bones affected by Inverse Kinematic Setup - Configuring the First Chain of Bones IK Locks

Starting with the First Bone in the Chain Visually read what the Local X/Y/Z Pole Vectors are and selectively Disable the corresponding X/Y/Z Lock IK Icons to ensure that all of the Bones rotate only on the Desired Movement Axes â†’ In the Case of the Green KED Splint, we only want this Bone Chain to be able to Rotate on the World Y Axis and thus must set the Locks for each of the 4 bones in the Chain

Step 6: Setting Bone Properties for Chain Bones affected by Inverse Kinematic Setup - Configuring the First Chain of Bones IK Locks Axis Limits

Now that the Bones rotating based on the Lock settings, we now need to set up the Limit and Min/Max Rotation Values for each of the Bones in the Chain so that we can have further control of how our Bones rotate in the Chain. Select a Bone â†’ In the Bone Properties tab of the Properties Panel â†’ Under Inverse Kinematics â†’ Lock IK â†’ Left Click on the Limit Checkbox for the Axis that is not Locked â†’ Now Enter the Minimum and Maximum Rotation Values and/or adjust the Value Slider until the Needed Rotation Value is achieved

  • Note: Limit Boxes for Axisâ€™s that are Locked will be Greyed out (since they are Locked and canâ€™t move on those Axisâ€™s) and only Axisâ€™s corresponding to the Unlocked Axisâ€™s will be able to be Toggled On and Off and have their Min/Max values adjusted

Step 7: Repeat the IK Setup Detailed Above for the Remainder of the Bone Chains needing IK

With the full workflow of setting up IK on Bone Chains detailed above - repeat the processes to set up the IK on the remaining Bone Chains of the Rig. For the purposes of this guide, a more condensed set of Screenshots will be provided showing the general setup of the other chains.

  • Note: You can easily duplicate the IKTarget bone and use 3D Cursor Snapping to position the duplicate on the other needed Bone Chains - just be sure to rename these duplicates so that they match the names of the Chains they correspond to
