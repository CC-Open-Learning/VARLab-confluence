VARLab : Testing @ VARLab

Created by Sana Javeed, last modified on Jun 07, 2024

A comprehensive guide designed to ensure the quality and usability of our software products at VARLab. This document outlines the testing procedures, methodologies, and best practices to be followed by our teams to verify the functionality, performance, and user experience of our software applications.

Purpose

This document serves as a roadmap for our testing efforts, providing clear instructions and guidelines for conducting various types of tests, including functional testing, usability testing, performance testing, and more.

By adhering to the practices outlined in this document, we aim to identify and address any defects or issues in our software early in the development process, ensuring a smooth and seamless user experience upon release.

Intended Audience

This document is intended for use by our PIs (Principal Investigators), Developers, Game Designers, UX/UI Designer, Instructional Designers, VLT Counsel, and other stakeholders involved in the software development process. It serves as a reference guide to ensure consistency and thoroughness in our testing approach, ultimately contributing to the delivery of high-quality software products that delight our users.

We encourage all team members to familiarize themselves with the contents of this document and to actively participate in the testing process. By working together and leveraging the guidance provided here, we can achieve our shared goal of delivering exceptional software that exceeds user expectations.

Responsibility Matrix

The Responsibility Matrix below defines the roles and responsibilities in creating, executing, consulting or just being informed throughout the testing process.

Responsible -R

Definition: The person or team responsible for performing the testing activities.

Responsibilities:

  • Execution of tests cases

  • Logging defects and issues

  • Contribute to the development of goals, test cases, scenarios and/or script

  • Ensuring that testing activities align with project requirements and timelines.

Accountable -A

Definition: The individual who has ultimate ownership and decision-making authority for the testing process and outcomes.

Responsibilities:

  • Creation and approval of goals, test cases, scenarios and/or script

  • Creation and definition of success criteria

  • Approving test strategies and plans.

  • Making final decisions on whether the software is ready for next phase.

  • Overseeing the overall testing process

Consult - C

Definition: The individuals or groups that provide subject matter expertise or are consulted for their insights during the testing process

Responsibilities:

  • Offering input on test case design

  • Providing domain-specific knowledge to enhance the testing process

  • Collaborating with the team to ensure alignment with business requirements

  • Providing feedback on the testing strategies

Inform - I

Definition: The individuals or groups who need to be kept informed about the progress and outcomes of the testing activities

Responsibilities:

  • Receiving updates on testing status, changes and results

  • Being informed of any issues, risks or critical findings

  • Providing feedback on testing outcomes from a broader perspective

Glossary

  1. Functional Testing : To validate if functionalities conform with all the requirements. It describes what an application does. It can be done manual/automation. Eg: check the functionality of a login button

  2. Non-Functional Testing: To validate the performance, reliability, usability, efficiency, & scalability of the application. It describes how an application works. Eg: if more people logged into the application at the same, how does it work

  3. Unit Testing: To test individual components of the software

  4. Integration Testing: To ensure that the entire system functions properly in an integrated system i.e. the hardware and software environment.

  5. Regression Testing: To confirm that the changes/ bug fixes have not negatively impacted the other features. Eg: Adding a ‘remember user_name’ checkbox as a feature later in development in the login page does not prevent user from typing the password or impact any existing functionality of the login

  6. Smoke Testing: To verify whether a new build is bug-free or not. Smoke Testing needs to be performed whenever there are some new features or capabilities added to the software, and a new build is delivered.

  7. Requirements Validation: Informal/ Formal testing where Content Practitioners (e.g. faculty and students) can focus on using the simulation for educational purposes, validating content accuracy, and providing feedback aligned with specific learning outcomes. They contribute where their expertise in content and learning objectives is most valuable.

  8. Security Testing: Evaluate the simulation's security measures to protect data and ensure system integrity, aligning with their expertise in system administration and DevOps.

  9. Performance Testing: Measures the software's responsiveness, stability, and scalability under various conditions - load testing for web usage & VR headset performance

  10. Data Integrity testing: Ensures data remains accurate and consistent during storage, retrieval, and processing.

  11. Network and Recovery Testing: Assesses how the software performs under varying network conditions, including low bandwidth and high latency.

  12. Compatibility Testing: Ensures the software functions correctly on different platforms, browsers, and devices.

  13. Usability Testing: To test the functionality for efficiency and effectiveness by observing real users as hey attempt to complete the tasks.
