USABILITY TESTING

Context: Feedback from Chris Harnock (CE)after the testing on Nov 8 followed by a lab to learn the impact of the simulation on the hands-on lab. He divided the students into groups.

  • High Performers: Students who performed well in the simulation required minimal guidance, finding the simulation helpful. They demonstrated improved understanding in the lab, needing fewer confirmations and showing confidence in their decisions, such as correctly recalling details about staples.

  • Middle Performers: Students in the mid-range showed slight improvement in their lab performance after using the simulation. While their hand skills may still need development, they asked fewer questions and exhibited increased confidence in moving through the installation process.

  • Low Performers: Students who struggled in the lab also faced challenges in the simulation, indicating that the simulation alone may not be enough to support this group. This group are the ones who do not ask questions in class or lab.

Students asked fewer questions - with the questions being at a higher level, showing high levels of task comprehension than the previous groups.

NOTE: These were the results of a 1 hour DLX exposure the day before the hands-on lab. Further investigation would be helpful like extending the timeline of the students DLX play before the lab might get us more insights.

Low visibility of items against background (Low contrast)

Some smaller items (such as the micron filters or zip ties) overlap other components when exploding from bags, pockets or compartments. This breaks the normal pattern for most items and can be confusing or unexpected to the player.

AMB

Blur or darken the background for a high visibility of items
