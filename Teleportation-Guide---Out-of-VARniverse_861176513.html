VARLab : Teleportation Guide - Out of VARniverse

Created by Brodin Collins-Robb, last modified on Apr 07, 2024

Now that you have set up your teleportation stone Teleportation Stone Guide in VARniverse, it's time to set up the teleportation to/from your project!

You must set up your teleportation stone in VARniverse BEFORE following this tutorial!

If your teleportation stone is not created yet, please refer to this guide: Teleportation Stone Guide

 Instructions

To begin, you will need your project to be inside the VARniverse's project assets tab under Mini-Games and your semester year.

Ensure to place all of your project assets into a unique folder for your project!

If you created your project in VARniverse, ensure it is placed in the correct directory.

If you created your project outside of VARniverse, you can drag and drop the entire contents of your project assets folder into the correct directory in VARniverse.

Sometimes when adding a new project to VARniverse the project that is imported may no longer work and/or throw unusual errors like ‘the type or namespace name does not exist’.

To solve these potential errors ensure that the VARniverse packages match the imported projects packages (Window → Package Manager → Packages: Unity Registry → and search for the name listed in the error and click install)

Ensure to add specific input controls to match your projects inputs (Edit → Project Settings → Input Manager)

  1. Ensure all your project assets are in the VARniverse assets folder described above.

  2. Find your teleportation stone in the project hierarchy and select it.

  3. In the Inspector tab, find the 'Teleport (Script)' it should look something like this:

  1. In the projects hierarchy, find the 'Mini Game Info Toggle Instruction' game object under 'UI Hub World→Canvas' (Rember uses the triangle on the left of the game objects to expand them to show more game objects).

  1. Now, by selecting back onto your teleportation stone and looking at the 'Teleportation (Script)' from Step 3, drag and drop the 'Mini Game Info Toggle Instruction' from Step 4 into the 'Teleport Instructions' 'None (Game Object)' field.

  1. In the projects hierarchy, find the 'Teleport info instruction' game object under 'UI Hub World→Canvas'

  1. Now, by selecting back onto your teleportation stone and looking at the 'Teleportation (Script)' from Step 3, drag and drop the 'Teleport info instruction' from Step 6 into the 'Toggle Instructions' 'None (Game Object)' field.

  1. In the project hierarchy, find the 'Main Player' game object.

  1. Now, by selecting back onto your teleportation stone and looking at the 'Teleportation (Script)' from Step 3, drag and drop the 'Main Player' from Step 8 into the 'Player' 'None (Game Object)' field.

  1. In the projects hierarchy, find the 'Dialogue Box' game object under 'UI Hub World→Canvas'

  1. Now, by selecting back onto your teleportation stone and looking at the 'Teleportation (Script)' from Step 3, drag and drop the 'Dialogue Box' from Step 10 into the 'Player' 'None (Game Object)' field.

  1. In the projects hierarchy, find the 'GameManager' game object.

  1. Now, by selecting back onto your teleportation stone and looking at the 'Teleportation (Script)' from Step 3, drag and drop the 'GameManager' from Step 12 into the 'Player' 'None (Game Object)' field.

Before continuing, you must ensure that in the project you will add to VARniverse, your scene name matches the name of your teleportation stone exactly!

  1. Navigate to the scene of your project from the Project → Assets → Mini-Game → and your semester and year and open (double click) your scene file like the scene file shown in the screenshot above.

  2. Then, in the top menu of Unity with your project scene open, go to File → Build Settings and click on ‘Add Open Scenes’ in the windows that popped open. This will add the scene you currently have open to the build list for VARniverse.

  1. While still having your project open, you want to build your project's lighting so that it can be loaded while switching scenes. This means you will need to go to Window → Rendering → Lighting, and in the window that pops open, select the ‘Generate Lighting’ button. (This can take some time to render)

At this point, when you walk up to your teleportation stone in VARniverse and select the 'f' key while looking at the stone, you should teleport into your scene.

Want to make your minigame accessible in VR mode?

If you would like your mini-game to be accessible in VR mode, you will need to add the ‘VR Check for interact teleport’ script to your teleportation stone.

This script should already be added to your teleport stone, but if it is not:

  • Find this script under the main scripts folder of the project

  • Drag the script under the Teleport script already on your Teleport stone

Click the VR Triggerbutton field and add the XRI RightHand INteraction FKeyFuncInVR

This should look like the following:

This guide only walks through teleporting from VARniverse to your project. To cover a guide on teleporting from your project back to VARniverse, please see this guide: Teleportation Guide - Into VARniverse
